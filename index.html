Work Experience

Mesh Engineering & Software Co.
August, 2021 - now

After a long hiatus caused by the pandemic, I began working as an Advanced Analysis & Simulation Specialist at Mesh Engineering & Software Co. However, I didn't know back then that this profession would become my dream job. Although it seemed only like Structural Analysis Engineering by the title, my work mainly involved programming. I started developing a beam analysis program using the finite element method for Turk Loydu under the TUBITAK R&D project. We utilized an open-source finite element solver called Code Aster. This project allowed me to gain profound insights into structural analysis and advanced usage scenarios of Code Aster solver, simultaneously enhancing my coding skills.

During this period, I broadened my horizons on how to write more efficient code. I realized the significant importance of object-oriented programming in such extensive projects. I honed my ability to design classes and work with interfaces in object-oriented programming. Thanks to my work, I had the privilege of collaborating closely with our manager, Mr. Yaar Gul. He was the most experienced Shipbuilding Engineer I had ever encountered in Turkey. His expertise extended across various domains, ranging from structural analysis and CFD to ship stability and programming. Moreover, he held revolutionary ideas within the industry. While developing the program, he provided valuable guidance, establishing numerous requirements and determining the program's functioning. With his exceptional ideas and my adept programming knowledge, we created a highly capable beam analysis program, Fem Beam Analyzer. We delivered it to Turk Loydu, and it will not only be used within Turk Loydu and Mesh Engineering but also be available for sale. It has applications not only in the Shipbuilding sector but also in all fields dealing with structural analysis. Despite having a user-friendly interface that even non-analysts can use, the program boasts advanced analysis customization capabilities, surpassing any other beam program available in the market.

When I first joined the company, the software team consisted solely of me. However, due to my success, the company management decided to expand the team, and we added two new teammates. As a team, we began developing a program that would provide solutions for finite element analysis, which was the second phase of the Turk Loydu R&D project. With this program, our company's analysis tasks would accelerate, and we would no longer need to pay high licensing fees for analysis programs like Ansys and Abaqus. Furthermore, Turk Loydu would use and market this program. It would also enable Turk Loydu to become a member of IACS (International Association of Classification Societies) thanks to this program. Working on this program enriched my knowledge of structural analyses for ships. I acquired insights into free vibration, forced vibration, and fluid-structure interaction modeling. Additionally, I learned how to perform these analyses using Code Aster. To validate the program, I compared its outputs with those from Ansys and Abaqus, which not only helped me master these programs but also identify their strengths and weaknesses alongside our own solver.
